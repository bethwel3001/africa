These Terms & Conditions ("T&C") constitute a legally binding agreement between AUSP.Africa ("the Organiser") and any individual or entity ("the Participant") who registers for, attends, sponsors, partners with, or otherwise participates in the International Pan-African Youth Conference 2026 ("the Conference"). 1
Definitions & scope
All participants
1.1 Definitions
In these T&C: "Conference" means IPAYC 2026, to be held in Nairobi, Kenya in October 2026. "Organiser" means AUSP.Africa and its authorised representatives. "Participant" means any person or entity engaging with the Conference in any capacity, including but not limited to delegates, speakers, panellists, sponsors, partners, VIPs, observers, media, volunteers, and facilitators. "Agreement" means these T&C together with any supplementary agreements, letters of intent, or Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) entered into with the Organiser.
1.2 Scope
These T&C apply to all Conference-related activities, including in-person attendance in Nairobi, virtual participation, pre-conference engagements, side events, social functions, media coverage, and any digital platforms operated by the Organiser in connection with IPAYC 2026.
1.3 Amendments
The Organiser reserves the right to amend these T&C at any time. Participants will be notified of material changes via their registered email. Continued participation constitutes acceptance of amended terms.

Registration confirmation
A delegate's place is confirmed only upon receipt of full payment of the applicable registration fee ($50 standard / $70 for delegates aged 36+) and written confirmation from the Organiser. Submission of a registration form does not constitute a guaranteed place.
Age bracket & fee accuracy
Delegates must accurately declare their age bracket. Misrepresentation of age to obtain a lower fee tier will result in immediate cancellation of registration without refund and may be reported to the delegate's parent institution.
Position paper — delegates aged 36+
Senior delegates (36+) are required to submit a position paper on behalf of their declared country or organisation prior to the Conference. The paper must: (a) be submitted no later than 30 days before the Conference; (b) be original work; (c) address at least one thematic pillar of IPAYC 2026. Failure to submit a position paper will not entitle the delegate to a refund but may restrict access to the UN-model plenary debate.
Delegate conduct
Delegates agree to participate respectfully in all sessions, uphold the Pan-African values of the Conference, and comply with all instructions from the Organiser and venue management. Disruptive behaviour will result in removal without refund.
Goodie bag & t-shirt
Delegate materials including the goodie bag and branded t-shirt are distributed once at the registration desk on the first day of the Conference. Uncollected items are not replaceable and no cash equivalent will be offered.

Content standards

The Organiser reserves the right to withdraw a speaker invitation if submitted materials do not meet these standards.

Registration, fees & payments
All participants
3.1 Fee structure
Registration fees are as follows: Standard delegate (ages 15–35): $50 USD. Senior delegate (age 36+): $70 USD, inclusive of position paper and UN-model plenary debate seat. VIPs, accredited media, and invited speakers may attend at no registration cost as determined by the Organiser. Sponsor and partner fees are as per their respective agreements.
3.2 Payment methods
Accepted payment methods will be communicated via confirmation email. The Organiser accepts mobile money (M-Pesa), bank transfer, and approved digital payment platforms. All fees are stated in USD. Bank charges or conversion fees are the responsibility of the payer.
3.3 Payment deadline
Full payment must be received no later than 30 days before the Conference. Registrations unpaid by this date will be automatically cancelled, and the space may be offered to waitlisted participants.
3.4 Receipts
An official receipt will be issued within 5 working days of payment confirmation. Delegates requiring invoices for institutional reimbursement should request these at the time of registration.
4
Cancellation, transfers & refunds
All participants
4.1 Cancellation by participant
Cancellations must be submitted in writing to the Organiser. The following refund policy applies:
  • Cancellation 60+ days before Conference: 75% refund
  • Cancellation 30–59 days before Conference: 50% refund
  • Cancellation less than 30 days before Conference: no refund
  • No-show without prior notice: no refund
4.2 Transfer of registration
Delegates may transfer their registration to another eligible person at the same or higher fee tier with written notice to the Organiser no later than 14 days before the Conference. A transfer fee of $10 USD applies.
4.3 Cancellation by Organiser
If the Organiser cancels the Conference for reasons other than force majeure, all paid delegates will receive a full refund within 30 working days. If the Conference is postponed, registrations will be transferred to the new date unless a delegate requests a refund in writing within 14 days of the postponement announcement.

Code of conduct
All participants
Violation of the code of conduct may result in immediate removal from the Conference without refund, and may be reported to the participant's parent institution, government, or relevant professional body.
5.1 Respect & inclusion
All participants are expected to treat every individual at the Conference with dignity and respect, regardless of gender, age, nationality, ethnicity, religion, disability, sexual orientation, or socioeconomic background. IPAYC 2026 is a safe and inclusive space for all.
5.2 Prohibited conduct
The following are strictly prohibited at all Conference venues and events:
  • Harassment, intimidation, or threatening behaviour of any kind
  • Hate speech, discrimination, or derogatory language
  • Physical violence or threatening of violence
  • Unauthorised recording or photography of individuals
  • Possession or use of illegal substances
  • Theft or damage of Conference or venue property
  • Misrepresentation of one's identity, credentials, or organisation
  • Disruption of sessions, debates, or official proceedings
5.3 Reporting
Participants who experience or witness a code of conduct violation should report it immediately to any member of the Conference Organising Committee or at the designated Delegate Support Desk. All reports will be treated confidentially and investigated promptly.
5.4 Social media conduct
Participants must not publish false, defamatory, or misleading content about the Conference, its organisers, sponsors, partners, or fellow participants on any social media platform. The Organiser reserves the right to pursue legal remedies for reputational damage caused by bad-faith social media conduct.

Sponsorship & partnership compliance
Sponsors
6.1 Activation restrictions
Sponsors and partners may only activate agreed benefits within designated areas and time slots confirmed by the Organiser. Unsanctioned branding, distribution of materials, or promotional activities at Conference venues are prohibited and may result in immediate removal of benefits without refund.
6.2 Anti-greenwashing & ethics
Sponsors may not use the Conference association to make claims about their social or environmental credentials that are materially misleading. The Organiser reserves the right to publicly disassociate itself from any sponsor found to be engaged in unethical practices during the period of the partnership.
6.3 Exclusivity
Category exclusivity is available at Title and Platinum sponsor tiers only, as specified in the Sponsorship Packages document. Exclusivity applies to the immediate sector of the sponsor (e.g., a Title sponsor from the banking sector prevents the Organiser from signing another banking sponsor). Exclusivity terms must be expressly stated in the signed Sponsorship Agreement.

Privacy & data protection
All participants
9.1 Data collected
The Organiser collects personal data including name, email, phone number, country, age bracket, dietary requirements, health and accessibility information, professional background, and in some cases passport or national ID details for visa support. This data is collected for the purposes of Conference management, logistics, communication, and post-event reporting.
9.2 Data usage
Personal data will be used solely for Conference-related purposes and will not be sold to any third party. Data may be shared with: (a) the Conference venue for logistics; (b) government authorities as required by Kenyan law; (c) partner organisations with whom you have given consent for data sharing. 9.3 Data retention
Personal data will be retained for a maximum of 24 months after the Conference for reporting and follow-up purposes, after which it will be securely deleted. Participants may request deletion of their data at any time by writing to the Organiser.
9.4 Your rights
Participants have the right to: access their personal data; request corrections; request deletion; withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time. To exercise these rights, contact the Organiser at the official contact address provided.
9.5 Security
The Organiser employs appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or misuse. In the event of a data breach that materially affects participants, the Organiser will notify affected individuals within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

The Organiser will make reasonable efforts to accommodate dietary requirements and accessibility needs declared at registration. Late declarations (within 14 days of the Conference) cannot be guaranteed to be fulfilled.
